About me

Observations with the TMRT in 2016 October

With the Shanghai Tianma 65-m Radio Telescope (TMRT), we preformed a large survey related to carbon isotope ratios, including sources from the Galactic Center Region to the star forming outskirts of the Milky Way well beyond the Perseus Arm. The study is based on measurements of the C-band 110-111 (4.8 GHz) and Ku-band 211-212 (14.5 GHz) K-doublet lines of H2CO and the 110-111 (4.6 GHz) line of H213CO toward a large sample of 112 Galactic molecular clouds. We obtain a linear fit of 12C/13C = 5.08(1.10)DGC + 11.86(6.60), with a correlation coefficient of 0.58. DGC refers to Galactocentric distances.

Observations with the Effelsberg in 2018 February

Using the Effelsberg-100 m telescope, we performed a successful pilot survey on H2O maser emission toward a small sample of radio-bright Seyfert 2 galaxies with a redshift larger than 0.04 between 2016 January 28 and February 1. Our survey led to one new megamaser source and one additional possible detection, which reflects our success in selecting H2O megamaser candidates compared to previous observations. In 2018 February, we confirmed the possible detection which we found in 2016 as a new H2O megamaser, and 10 tentative masers were detected (Zhang et al. in preparation).
